About Philippine Heritage

The Philippines is rich in history, filled with centuries-old churches, forts, shrines, and cultural landmarks. These historical places tell stories of bravery, faith, colonization, and the Filipino spirit.

Apolinario Mabini Monument

This site is designated as a landmark because it serves as the primary commemorative space for the "Sublime Paralytic," one of the most influential intellectuals of the Philippine Revolution. It was established as a landmark through the collaboration of the National Historical Commission and local advocates to ensure that Mabini’s philosophical contributions were not overshadowed by military feats.

Cathedral Parish of Saint Paul the First Hermit

The Cathedral became a landmark due to its status as a "living" historical structure t hat survived the colonial period and multiple wars while maintaining its Spanish Baroque architectural integrity. It reached landmark status as the spiritual center of the Bajo de la Campana system, an urban planning method used by Spain to organize the population around the church. ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀

Fule-Malvar Ancestral Mansion

This mansion is a landmark because it is a rare, well-preserved example of the Bahay na Bato style, representing the fusion of Filipino, Spanish, and Chinese influences. It became an official landmark through its direct association with General Miguel Malvar, one of the last major revolutionary leaders to surrender during the Philippine-American War.

Gusaling Pamahalaan Lungsod ng San Pablo

It is recognized as a landmark because it has functioned as the continuous seat of governance through the city’s most significant administrative milestones, including its transition to a chartered city. It became a landmark through "functional history," as the site where local laws and public policies were crafted to shape the growth of the region.

San Pablo City Plaza Fountain

The fountain and the surrounding plaza became landmarks by virtue of the Spanish Leyes de las Indias (Laws of the Indies), which dictated that the center of town must be a public space for community interaction. It achieved landmark status through the passage of time and its preservation as a communal "living room." ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀ ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀

Jose P. Laurel Memorial House

This was the ancestral home of Jose P. Laurel, who served as the President of the Philippines during the Japanese Occupation in World War II. It is historically significant because it offers a glimpse into the life of a leader who had to navigate one of the most complex and controversial periods in Philippine history. The house is a "National Historical Landmark" and preserves the personal belongings, library, and memorabilia of the Laurel family.

Modesto Memorial Cultural Center

Named after a former Governor of Batangas and Associate Justice of the Supreme Court, this center is a landmark of local governance and civic pride. While it serves as a modern hub for culture and the arts, it is significant for honoring Judge Modesto Castillo’s contributions to the Philippine judiciary and his dedication to the people of Batangas. It reflects the region's tradition of producing high-level legal and political minds.

Saint John Evangelist Parish Church

Commonly known as the Tanauan Church, this is a focal point of the city's history. The original church was destroyed during the eruption of Taal Volcano in 1754, forcing the entire town to relocate to its current site. The present structure, built in the mid-19th century, is a prime example of Spanish-Filipino baroque architecture. It stands as a symbol of the community's resilience and its long-standing Catholic heritage.

Tanauan Museum

This museum serves as the "memory bank" of the city. It is historical because it archives the evolution of Tanauan from a lakeside settlement to a bustling city. It houses artifacts related to the town’s founding families, its agricultural roots (specifically in the citrus industry), and its role during the Philippine Revolution and World War II.

Apolinario Mabini Shrine

This is perhaps the most intellectually significant landmark in the region. It is the birthplace and final resting place of Apolinario Mabini, known as the "Sublime Paralytic" and the "Brains of the Revolution." ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀ ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀
